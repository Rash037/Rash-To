Not All Heroes Wear Capes (But They Do Carry Heavy Backpacks) 🦸‍♂️💭

Okay, real talk, I’ve got what people call Superhero Syndrome (aka the Superman Complex). Basically, I’m that person who feels like I need to save everyone, fix everything, and make sure nothing bad happens to my people. Like, if something’s coming their way, it better go through me first. Sounds like main character energy, right? Well… it’s actually lowkey exhausting.

Where It All Started 👨‍👩‍👦

I think it comes from my parents. They’re literally built different. They’ll help people even if they hate them, and they always drop this one line: “Agar hum bhi unke jaise ban jaye to unme aur hum me kya farq rahega” (If we become like them, then what’s the difference between us and them?). That one line pretty much tattooed itself on my brain. And boom, now I can’t just stand by when something wrong is happening.

School Flashbacks 📚

Back in school, I used to mess with bullies just to protect other kids. One time I even went against my own friends because they were teasing a girl. At that moment, I didn’t care about losing them, I cared about doing the right thing. The twist? That girl’s been one of my closest friends for the past 5 years. We still talk a lot. That one choice literally rewrote my story.

The Dark Side of Playing Hero 🖤

Here’s the stuff no one tells you: being a “hero” is heavy.

Whenever something bad happens to my people, I instantly blame myself. If someone says something mean to my friend, my brain goes: “If I was there, I would’ve protected her. It’s all my fault.” Even when it’s not. Then I end up hating myself, cursing myself, and ruining my day.

"If I can't save the one small girl in front of me, how can I become a hero who saves everyone?"

Deku, My Hero Acadmia

Final Thought 🌌

If you feel the same, remember this: you don’t have to take every hit to prove your worth. Protecting people also means protecting your own peace. Even heroes need to log off and recharge.

Sometimes the bravest thing to say is: “I can’t fix everything, but I’ll stand with you anyway.” 🤍
